## **Production \& Inventory Monitoring Dashboard (Power BI)**



##### **Project Overview**



This project was developed as part of my undergraduate work, where I worked with a real-world dataset provided by Miniwee, an online clothing brand. The goal was to build an interactive Power BI dashboard that could help monitor production and inventory operations more efficiently. Instead of relying on manual tracking, the dashboard provides a centralized and visual way to understand how different parts of the business are performing. The dashboard is designed to support data-driven decision-making, although the current version uses historical 2024 data.



##### **What I aimed to do**

##### 

* Track production and inventory in a more structured and visual way
* Identify issues like low stock and out-of-stock products
* Understand sales trends and profitability
* Analyze supplier performance and delivery delays
* Explore customer behavior and demand patterns



##### **Dataset**



* Provided by Miniwee
* Contains 5000+ records and 40+ attributes
* Stored in Excel format



The dataset includes information related to:



* Production and inventory levels
* Sales and revenue
* Customers and orders
* Suppliers and delivery timelines
* Returns and logistics



##### **Tools I used**



* Power BI for building dashboards and visualizations
* Power Query for cleaning and transforming data
* DAX for creating calculated measures and KPIs
* Excel as the data source



##### **How I approached the project**



###### **Data Preparation**



I started by importing the dataset into Power BI and cleaning it using Power Query. This involved handling missing values, removing duplicates and standardizing formats.



###### **Data Modeling**

###### 

I created a star schema model and defined relationships between different tables to make analysis more efficient.



###### **KPI Creation using DAX**



I created multiple measures such as:



* Total Sales and Profit
* Stock Turnover Ratio
* Return Rate
* On-time vs Late Deliveries
* Low Stock and Out-of-Stock Products



###### **Dashboard Development**



I designed multiple dashboards using:



* KPI cards
* Bar, line and pie charts
* Filters and slicers for better interactivity



##### **What the dashboard covers**



###### **1. Sales Performance**



* Monthly sales and profit trends
* Best and least performing products
* Category-wise sales contribution



###### **2. Inventory Insights**



* Stock levels across warehouses
* Identification of low-stock and out-of-stock products
* Inventory movement trends



###### **3. Supplier Performance**



* Procurement spending
* Delivery timelines
* Cost vs selling price analysis



###### **4. Customer Analysis**



* Customer acquisition channels
* Repeat customers
* Subscription insights



###### **5. Logistics \& Delivery**



* Shipping costs
* On-time vs delayed deliveries
* Delivery partner performance



###### **6. Profitability**



* Revenue vs expenses
* Profit margins by category
* Impact of product returns



##### **Key things I found**



* A significant number of products (1700+) were out of stock
* Certain suppliers had higher delivery delays, affecting operations
* Some product categories consistently performed better than others
* Returns had a noticeable impact on overall profitability
* Sales showed clear seasonal trends



##### **Outcome**



This dashboard helped in:



* Getting a clear view of operations in one place
* Identifying inefficiencies in inventory and supply chain
* Understanding business performance through data
* Supporting better decision-making
